Sports Offered

Baseball

March through June.

Ages: 5 to 12.

Teams: T-Ball (5-6yo), Rookies (7-8yo), Minors (9-10yo), Majors (11-12yo)

Organized through Kearsarge Valley Cal Ripken League.

Registration opens in January.

Softball

March through June.

Ages: 6 to 12.

Teams: U-8, U-10, U-12

Organized through Kearsarge Mountain South.

Registration opens in January.

Spring Soccer Clinic

May

Pre-K to Grade 5

Wednesdays for four weeks after school at Simonds Elementary in May. Hosted by Caramba Soccer Skills Camp. Pre-K/K and grade-school soccer-based enrichment program.

Register on TeamSnap

Soccer

September through mid-October.

Ages: 4 to 12.

Teams: PreK/K Coed, 1/2 Coed, 3/4 Boys, 3/4 Girls, 5/6 Boys, 5/6 Girls

Organized through Merrimack Valley Soccer League. Volunteer coaches.

Pre-K/K is practice only. 1/2 plays locally with Bradford and New London, games on Saturday mornings. Practices begin the first week of school.

3/4 and 5/6 play in MVSL with several local towns, games on weekdays, and Saturday afternoons. Practices start late August.

Registration opens in July.

Winter Basketball

November 15 to January.

Grade 2 through 5.

Wednesdays 6:30-7:30pm at Simonds School gym. Skills coaching and pick-up games.

Registration opens in October.

Ski/Snowboard Program

December through February.

First grade to 18.

Thursday evenings for five weeks following Winter Vacation. Organized through WYSA, instruction provided by Pats Peak, followed by one hour of free-skiing with WYSA chaperones. No transportation provided this year due to shortage of bus drivers.

Use registration code kearsarge2024.

Early-bird pricing ends Nov 15.

Contact Glen Moyer (glenmoyer603@yahoo.com) for more information or to apply for assistance.

Registration opens in November, through Pats Peak.

Warner Youth Sports Association (WYSA) is a charitable non-profit organization dedicated to giving every child in Warner the opportunity to participate in local sports, regardless of their ability to pay. We are an all-volunteer organization, funded by local business donations, fundraisers, and individual registration fees.
